# How to Use the Enhanced Curriculum

## What's New

The Real World Cirricullum now features a **comprehensive curriculum progression** for each topic, from **Basics → Intermediate → Advanced**. Each level builds on previous knowledge and adds real-world depth.

---

## How to Navigate

### Step 1: Choose a Topic
Visit the homepage and select any topic (e.g., **Personal Finance**, **Law for Daily Life**, etc.)

### Step 2: Select Your Level
You'll see three tabs at the top:
- **Basics** - Foundation knowledge you need first
- **Intermediate** - Practical application and deeper scenarios  
- **Advanced** - Mastery, strategy, and teaching others

### Step 3: Work Through Each Module
Each level contains:
- **Learning Modules** - Actionable steps you can take immediately
- **Core Concepts** - Key ideas to understand
- **Key Terminology** - Words and phrases explained
- **Common Questions** - Answers to typical concerns
- **References** - Resources to learn more
- **Recovery Guidance** - What to do if things go wrong
- **Quiz** - Test your understanding

### Step 4: Take the Quiz
Complete the quiz at the bottom of each level to verify your understanding. Your score is saved locally.

### Step 5: First Action
Before moving to the next level, complete the **First Action** task—it's your immediate next step.

---

## Recommendation: How to Progress

**For New Learners:**
1. Start with **Basics** and complete all modules
2. Practice the **First Action** for 1-2 weeks
3. Move to **Intermediate** once comfortable
4. Take your time—depth beats speed

**For Experienced Learners:**
1. Skim **Basics** quickly if familiar
2. Focus on **Intermediate** and **Advanced**
3. Use **Common Questions** to fill gaps
4. Reference **Recovery Guidance** when needed

**For Specific Problems:**
1. Jump to **Intermediate** or **Advanced** tabs
2. Use "When Things Go Wrong" section
3. Check **References** for detailed help

## Tips for Success

### 1. **Start with Basics**
Foundation matters. Even if familiar, the Basics level provides the framework.

### 2. **Do the First Action**
It's intentionally small and doable. Doing it builds confidence.

### 3. **Read the "When Things Go Wrong" Section**
You'll learn more from problems you might face than happy paths.

### 4. **Use References**
If a concept feels unclear, check References for deeper learning.

### 5. **Revisit When Needed**
Come back to a topic when facing actual situations. Real context deepens learning.

### 6. **Share What You Learn**
Teaching others solidifies your own understanding.

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I need to do all three levels?**  
A: No, choose based on your needs. Basics + Intermediate covers most scenarios. Advanced is for mastery.

**Q: Can I skip directly to Advanced?**  
A: You can, but Basics and Intermediate provide essential context and vocabulary.

**Q: If I get a quiz wrong, what should I do?**  
A: Review the concept in the "Key Terminology" or "Common Questions" sections. The tip with the question usually explains the idea.

**Q: How long does each level take?**  
A: Typically 1-3 hours, depending on how deeply you explore references and do the practical work.

**Q: Can I download or print the content?**  
A: Not built-in, but you can copy/paste to documents. Let us know if this would be helpful!
